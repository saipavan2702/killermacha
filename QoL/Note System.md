Keep the vault easy to use: capture quickly, clarify later, and organize by meaning.

## Flow

1. **Capture** unfinished thoughts and links in [[Inbox]].
2. **Clarify** the useful idea in your own words.
3. **Organize** it under the topic where you would look for it later.
4. **Connect** it to a few genuinely related notes.
5. **Review** important notes and improve them when they become useful again.

## Shape of a Learning Note

```md
# Clear Topic Name

> [!summary]
> A short explanation of the idea and why it matters.

## Key Ideas

## Details

## Example

## Recall

- A question that tests understanding.

## Related

- [[Upskill/SysDes/HLD/Caching|Caching]]

---

## References

- [Useful source](https://example.com) - What it supports.
```

Use only the sections the topic needs. Small notes do not need a full template.

## Naming

- Name notes after the idea: `CAP Theorem.md`, not `Core.md`.
- Prefer familiar words over abbreviations.
- Use one topic per note when the topic can stand on its own.
- Use a folder-named map such as `Math.md` or `System Design.md` instead of `Index.md`.
- Keep links descriptive and avoid duplicate copies of the same explanation.

## Adapted Methods

- **Second Brain / PARA:** the current roots already act as lightweight areas and resources. Add more folders only when retrieval becomes difficult.
- **Evergreen notes:** concept-sized notes with clear titles are easier to link and improve over time.
- **Progressive summarization:** put the shortest useful explanation first; retain deeper examples below it.
- **Cornell method:** add recall questions to study notes so review is active rather than passive.
